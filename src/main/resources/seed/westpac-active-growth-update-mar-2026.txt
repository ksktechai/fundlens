Westpac Active Growth Fund — Quarterly Fund Update, period ended 31 March 2026

What happened to the fund this quarter
The fund returned -4.1% after fees and tax for the year to 31 March 2026, against a
market index return of -3.5%. The largest detractor was international equities, where
falling US technology valuations reduced returns by approximately 2.8 percentage points.
Australasian equities were broadly flat, while fixed interest contributed +0.6%.

Fees
The total annual fund charge is 1.05% of net asset value, comprising a 0.95% management
fee and 0.10% in-fund costs. There are no contribution, withdrawal, or performance fees.

What the fund invests in
Target investment mix: 55% international equities, 20% Australasian equities,
20% fixed interest, 5% cash. Actual mix at quarter end: 54.2% international equities,
21.1% Australasian equities, 19.5% fixed interest, 5.2% cash.

Key facts
Fund size: $2.15 billion. Number of investors: 98,000. Risk indicator: 4 out of 7.
The five-year average annual return after fees and tax is 6.2%.
